Croissants

Back to Main Page

Ingredients

For Dough

  • 2 (0.25 oz) packets active dry yeast (4 1/2 tsp)
  • 1 cup lukewarm water (about 110 degrees F)
  • 3 1/2 cups (420 g) bread flour, plus more for work surface
  • 1/4 cup (50 g) granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 6 tbsp vegan butter, softened
  • 1 cup vegan butter, slightly softened
  • 2 tbsp nondairy milk
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup or agave

For Fillings

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 tsp almond extract
  • Chocolate
  • Vegan pistachio butter
  • Vegan cheese slice
  • Vegan ham, diced
  • Poppy seeds, for topping
  • Powdered sugar, for topping
  • Almond slivers, for topping

Instructions

  1. Add the lukewarm water to the bowl of a stand mixer. Whisk in the yeast and allow to proof until foamy (about 5 minutes).
  2. Add in the granulated sugar, bread flour, salt, and the softened vegan butter. Mix until dough comes together in one large mass. At this point it will look very rough and shaggy.
  3. Knead for 6-8 minutes until dough is smooth and elastic. It should clear the sides of the bowl. Dough should bounce back when poked gently. If the dough seems overly wet or sticky, gradually knead in extra flour 1 tablespoon at a time until the right consistency is achieved.
  4. At this point, try the "window test". Stretch your dough out as thin as it can go. If you can get it thin enough for light to come through without it breaking, it's ready!
  5. Lightly oil a bowl and place the dough inside. Cover the bowl. Allow to rise until doubled (about 1 hour). If it's chilly in your kitchen, it might take a bit longer.
  6. Punch down the dough to remove any large air bubbles. Transfer to a clean surface. Gently stretch the dough out with your hands and fold it into a square shape.
  7. Transfer the dough to a sealable container. It will continue to rise, so make sure there is a little extra space for it to expand. I like to to use an 8×8” baking dish with a lid — this will help to keep it in a square shape. You can also tightly wrap it in plastic wrap. Place the dough in the fridge and allow to rise for at least 2 hours, OR overnight.
  8. Next we will want to shape 1 cup of butter into a flat, square slab. I like to do this by taking a large piece of parchment and folding it into a 6×6" square shape. Place the butter inside the square and fold the edges of the parchment over to enclose it. Then use a rolling pin to pound and roll the butter to fill the parchment square. Roll over it to form it into a nice flat, even slab. Place the butter slab in the fridge to chill.
  9. Once the dough has chilled for 2 hours OR overnight, it is ready to laminate. First bring your butter slab out of the fridge and place it on the counter so it has a few minutes to soften just *slightly.* Our aim is to have the butter slightly pliable but not soft — similar in texture to the chilled dough.
  10. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface and roll it out into a large rectangle, roughly 8"x16". (It needs to be slightly larger than twice the length of the butter slab.) Use a pastry brush to dust off any excess flour from the surface of the dough. Place the butter slab on one side, leaving a border, then fold the dough over to envelop the butter. Pinch around the edges to seal in the butter tightly.
  11. Roll the dough out to roughly 24×9". Use a pastry brush to dust off excess flour. Perform one single turn: Fold the dough in 3rds like a letter.
  12. Place the dough back in the sealed container (or wrap it tightly with parchment/plastic). Place in the fridge and chill for at least 1 hour before proceeding. This is necessary to keep the butter from softening too much, and it will allow the gluten to relax so the dough will not resist being rolled out again.
  13. Once the dough has chilled, roll it out again to 24×9". Again, use your pastry brush to dust off excess flour. Now perform a double turn: Fold both ends of the dough to meet in the center, then fold in half again like a book. Cover dough and refrigerate for 1 hour OR overnight to allow the gluten to fully relax.
  14. Lightly dust your work surface with flour and roll dough out to just over 20" wide and 18" tall. Use a very sharp knife to trim the left and right edges just slightly, so they are straight and crisp.
  15. Now cut the dough into 5 long rectangles, each about 4 inches wide. Then cut each rectangle diagonally from corner to corner, creating 10 long triangles. Each triangle will be one croissant.
  16. If you're using fillings, prepare them now. Mix almond flour, sugar, butter, and almond extract for almond filling. Place the almond filling, chocolate, pistachio butter, or ham and cheese in the croissant.
  17. Working one at a time, use your pastry brush to dust excess flour off the dough. Cut a small slit at the base of each triangle, then gently pull to elongate the piece of dough even more. Now gently roll your dough into a crescent shape.
  18. Place on a baking tray (lined with parchment or a silicone baking mat) with the point or seam of the croissant on the bottom.
  19. Lightly cover the croissants with plastic or a slightly damp tea towel. Allow croissants to rise at room temperature for 1-2 hours, until doubled in size. They should look noticeably puffy and should jiggle slightly if you shake the baking tray.
  20. Preheat oven to 400°F toward the end of the rising time.
  21. Optionally, you can whisk together 1 tablespoon agave or maple syrup with 2 tablespoons plant milk to make a vegan egg wash substitute. Lightly brush the croissants with this mixture to help them achieve a glossy finish.
  22. To distinguish the different fillings, top the almond crossiants with powdered sugar and slivered almonds, the chocolate with powdered sugar or chocolate drizzle, the ham and cheese with poppy seeds, and the pistachio with pistachios.
  23. Optionally, you can whisk together 1 tablespoon agave or maple syrup with 2 tablespoons plant milk to make a vegan egg wash substitute. Lightly brush the croissants with this mixture to help them achieve a glossy finish.
  24. Allow the croissants to cool on the baking tray for about 5 minutes before transferring to a wire cooling rack.
  25. Enjoy!
  26. Note: You can freeze the croissants after shaping them but before letting them rise. Then defrost in the fridge overnight when ready to use.
